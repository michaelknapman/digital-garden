List of all financial risks. 
Having too much of a single type of risk will lead to a high chance of a financial catastrophe. You must be exposed to many types of small risks to reduce the magnitude of the few large risks.
Some of the most catastrophic risks are caused by inaction, so you must choose your own risks, else fate will choose risks for you without your consent.
This list of financial risks is ordered from most common to least common.

Low wage risk. Not earning enough money is catastrophic. A major goal of life is earning enough money (resources) to support yourself and anyone else you want. This means you must invest in education, networking, etc.
Losing your job risk.
Disability risk. Getting disabled before the age of 65 means the loss of income and therefore an early retirement. If you get disabled at age 30, you WILL catastrophically run out of money before you die. Mitigate with disability insurance.
Loan & Mortgage interest rate risk.
Spending too much money risk. AKA not saving enough money risk
Leaving too much money in chequing and savings risk. (aka opportunity risk)
Longevity risk.
Inflation risk.
Market risk.
Bond default risk.
Bond interest rate risk. Government interest rate changes cause wild fluctuations in bond prices, and long term changes in bond coupon rates. Mitigates longevity risk by adding expected return. Mitigated by using bonds of multiple durations. 


List of risks that are easily avoided and do not mitigate other risks. Should therefore always be avoided.

Company specific risk. Stocks and bonds purchased from a single company have a high chance to have a dramatic loss of value. Mitigate by holding multiple companies.
Country specific risk. Stocks and bonds purchased from a single country of origin are riskier than if purchased from mulitple countries. Mitigated by using international diversification. 
Liquidity risk. Defined as when you have assets, but you can't convert it into cash when you need it. GICs are the most common cause of liquidity risk, as you sign a contract that locks money away for a fixed period of time. Also high minimum amount chequing accounts count too, as you cannot drop below $5000 without getting hit with fees. Therefore, use no-strings-attached chequing accounts, and use safe government bill funds such as CBIL which are insured by the government similar to GICs.


Lists of risks that cannot be mitigated and can therefore be ignored.

Currency risk. Exchange rates fluctuate and create volatility. Exposing yourself to currency risk is often a prerequisite for investing. Mitigates country specific risk. Cannot be mitigated.
Tax risk. Your government can change tax rules at any time, which may encourage you to hold on to assets longer, or sell assets sooner. Cannot be mitigated except by having a diverse set of accounts including Taxable, RRSP, TFSA, FHSA.
Regulatory risk. Your government can change the rules of investing at any time, and may force you to sell assets, freeze or seize a portion of your assets. This rarely occurs because it causes economic destructions. Occurred during past communist revolutions. Cannot be mitigated.
Political risk. Other countries may at any time force you to sell assets, or freeze or seize a portion of your assets. This rarely occurs, because such a move causes mutual economic destruction between countries. Occurred during wars such as the Invasion of Ukraine, and also occured during Nigeria's foreign exchange crisis of 2020-2024. Cannot be mitigated.
